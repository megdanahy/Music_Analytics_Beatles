From this anlaysis, I have noted three key insights:
1. Their music's energy generally decreased as The Beatles' recording career continued.
2. Their individual albums had wider energy distribution, meaning the energy per album remained somewhat variable. This could mean they treid to appeal to a wider audience with differing opinions on high energy songs.
3. Distribution of danceability remained somewhat concentrated, but was more variable on their more conceptual albums which makes sense. The widest distribution can be found on The White Album, and Yellow Submarine-- two albums that were revered for being off the beaten path, and more story reliant.
